A Year of Inactivity & Procrastination

February 2, 2019 2 minutes read
random • confession

I have been meaning to write a post for a long time, but every time I was held down by some other activity, or so I thought. Procrastination is the bane of most inexperienced writers, and in my case, it also reflected in “my ways” of doing assignments and projects since last two years. I am not saying that procrastination is the only cause of my inactivity, but it does contribute to it a lot. We seem to complain that we don’t have time to write, but when do have time, we while it away and wonder how all the time passed.

One of the main causes of my idleness has been the courseload I took in the last two semesters. From the first year, I was habitual of taking six or more classes and didn’t let go of this habit until recently. Even during the recess, when I did get time, the excuse of taking “too many” courses reinforced my procrastinating self to take over.

However, whenever I did shift my attention to blog, I always somehow found a “better” technology to use or a “better” way of doing things. For example, I have changed domain name twice, discarded Jekyll in favor of Hugo, tried multiple themes and abandoned Cloudflare completely since my last blog. Well, why? I would simply answer by saying it was fun. And it did satisfy my appetite for trying and learning something new.

I do not get the inspiration to write during my free time as I generally get bogged down reading some book or listening to some exciting podcast(mostly history related). When I do get inspiration to write, it is on the most inappropriate moments like in the middle of the night, in the bathroom, etc.

Hopefully, with the time I will learn to write even when I am not “free” and/or inspired. This post is indeed an attempt towards that direction and knowing my weakness well, I shall try to fix it in time.
